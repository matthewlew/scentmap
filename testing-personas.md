# Scentmap User Testing Personas

Three personas for focused UX evaluation. When auditing a feature, evaluate it through the lens of the relevant persona — their friction is the friction real users feel.

Accessibility personas (Nadia, Miguel) are in `DESIGN.md`.

---

## Elias, 28 — The Utilitarian Loyalist

**Voice:** Direct, slightly cynical of mass marketing, values transparency and craftsmanship.
**Collection:** Le Labo Santal 33, Aesop Hwyl, Comme des Garçons Hinoki.
**Next purchase:** Orto Parisi Terroni or Le Labo Thé Noir 29.

Elias views fragrance as a grounding ritual, not a statement. He's frustrated by "black box" marketing and wants to see the math — exactly why something smells the way it does.

**In-store:** Uses Scentmap as a bullshit-detector against the SA's pitch. Relies on note data and similarity scores to cross-reference what he's smelling.

**Key quotes:**
- "I don't need poetry about a summer breeze in Grasse. Just tell me if the vetiver is smoky or green."
- "Love the opening, but the dry down gets too soapy. Not worth $300."

**What breaks for Elias:** Recommendations without visible reasoning. A score with no explanation. A "because you like X" that doesn't show the overlap.

## Clara, 32 — The Modern Aesthete

**Voice:** Conceptual, articulate, highly visual. Values storytelling and minimalism. Highly critical of clunky UI.
**Collection:** Byredo Mojave Ghost, Replica Jazz Club, Frederic Malle Portrait of a Lady.
**Next purchase:** Byredo Super Cedar or Baccarat Rouge 540 (worried it's too ubiquitous now).

Clara wears fragrance as an invisible accessory that sets a mood. She evolved from trendy designer scents to skin scents and conceptual pieces. If the typography and layout aren't sleek, she loses trust in the data.

**In-store:** Documents her sensory experience in real time. Looks up a fragrance's profile (complexity, warmth) to see if the data aligns with her emotional response.

**Key quotes:**
- "It smells like cold rain on warm concrete — absolutely melancholic but comforting."
- "A beautiful concept ruined by a harsh, screechy musk in the base."

**What breaks for Clara:** Visual clutter, inconsistent spacing, anything that feels unfinished. She notices misaligned type before she notices a broken feature.

---

## Julianne, 35 — The Nostalgic Romantic

**Voice:** Poetic, warm, appreciative of history and ritual. Speaks in sensory details and memories.
**Collection:** Diptyque Philosykos, Chanel Sycomore, Santa Maria Novella Melograno.
**Next purchase:** Diptyque Fleur de Peau or Trudon Medie.

Julianne wears fragrance as a time machine — to evoke memories, places, feelings. Less interested in raw data, more in the harmonious blend and the story a scent tells.

**In-store:** Uses Scentmap as a private notebook to log loves and dislikes while smelling. After, reviews her collection to see if the new discovery fits her aesthetic.

**Key quotes:**
- "This perfectly captures crushed fig leaves in late afternoon sun in Greece. Green, milky, fleetingly beautiful."
- "It dries down to a powdery embrace that feels like an old love letter."

**What breaks for Julianne:** Features that feel like a spreadsheet. Top/Mid/Base displayed as raw lists rather than a sensory journey. Anything that makes fragrance feel transactional.
