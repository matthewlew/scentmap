Frequently Asked Questions

How does Scentmap compare fragrances?

Scentmap uses a multi-factor similarity algorithm. Each comparison scores family compatibility (40%), shared note overlap weighted by tier — base notes count most (30%), sillage proximity (10%), and shared fragrance roles (20%). The result is a 0–100% match score plus a breakdown of shared and unique notes, radar charts, and layering compatibility.

What fragrance brands does Scentmap cover?

Scentmap currently covers 180+ fragrances from 12 brands: Aesop, Byredo, Chanel, Dior, Diptyque, D.S. & Durga, Fueguia 1833, Le Labo, Loewe, Replica (Maison Margiela), Tom Ford, and Xinu. The database is regularly updated with new releases.

How do the fragrance quizzes work?

Each quiz asks 3–5 questions about your style, vibe, season preference, and note preferences. Your answers generate weighted tags that are scored against every fragrance in the database. The algorithm considers family match, note profile compatibility, sillage preferences, and role alignment to recommend your top 3 matches.

What are fragrance notes and why do they matter?

Fragrance notes are the individual scent ingredients in a perfume, organized into three tiers: top notes (first impression, 15–30 minutes), heart/middle notes (the core character, 1–3 hours), and base notes (the lasting foundation, 4+ hours). Understanding notes helps you predict whether you'll like a fragrance and find similar alternatives.

What is sillage and how is it measured?

Sillage (from the French word for "wake") describes how far a fragrance projects from the wearer. Scentmap rates sillage on a 0–10 scale: 0–3 is intimate (skin scent), 4–6 is moderate (arm's length), and 7–10 is powerful (room-filling). Sillage is different from longevity — a fragrance can last all day but sit close to the skin.

What fragrance families does Scentmap categorize?

Scentmap organizes fragrances into 9 families: Woody (sandalwood, cedar, vetiver), Floral (rose, jasmine, iris), Amber (labdanum, benzoin, warm resins), Citrus (bergamot, lemon, yuzu), Leather (suede, birch tar, smoky), Oud (agarwood-dominant), Green (galbanum, tea, herbs), Chypre (oakmoss, bergamot, patchouli), and Gourmand (vanilla, caramel, coffee).
